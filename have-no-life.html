What does it mean to "have no life"?

November 17, 2013 [ life ] #lifestyle Read in 3 min.

I began to hear this term since college. From my experience, it was used as an excuse for something or just a attempt to gain sympathy from peers (who doesn't want to play victim?). I was reading a post by an investment banker on Quora who confessed to become wealthy at the cost of having no life. He was trying to send a message that money is not everything, but that got me thinking... do I have a life? I do not watch TV or video games, but the only place I go on weekends is church. Otherwise, I just work or study or read articles on the web. I do things instead of sitting idly somewhere or force myself to meet people.

For personal questions, I think Yahoo Answer is the best place. Answers are simple, direct, and suited for everyone. One person said:

To me "HAVING LIFE" means, being contended with one's life, having self respect and dignity, having values and morals, and not interested to control, interfere and bother other's life or jealous of others. So even a poor person earning just to live have all those, then he has life.
While "Having no life" means, the one who is not contended with his/her own life, has no self esteem and has no respect for self and others and have low values and morals. Even a rich with relationships and family/friends doesn't have above, then he/she has no life

Here additional things that make me look like I do not have life.

  • I do not travel, but make exceptions for people I have not met for a long time.
  • I do not try to get laid.
  • I do not try to make my life seem interesting to others.

Here are things that I do so I can have a life.

  • I provide people tech support without any expectation.
  • I have few people me in my contact list, so I can get back to them right away.
  • I do not have Facebook. I do not have even have that many friends. But I recently celebrated my friend's wedding half way around the world. I could not go there myself, but I had a relative you was glad to show up on my behalf.
  • I try to be committed to people I see today, rather than a year ago.
  • I like to celebrate everyone's birthday together.
  • I live in a protective cell.
  • My family loves me to death, because I do what they ask of me and more.

I do not know if I work hard, but recently I was working late one night and my co-worker told me to go home because I look rest of the team look bad. So I just take my work laptop home now. If you think you are working HARD, you are not enjoying what you are doing. You should go to work dancing. Your work should be a playground. It should be a place where you get self-actualization and self-esteem.

But most important of all, your family is the indicator of a healthy relationship. Go watch click or It's a Wonderful Life. If you have a outstanding family relationship (that is built on a moral ground, not life mafia), you have life in my book. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise.
