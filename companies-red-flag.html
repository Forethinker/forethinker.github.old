Companies that raised red flags during my interview process

September 11, 2013 [ life ] #career Read in 3 min.

Hey you should take my experience with a grain of salt, but these are the rules I will bind by in the future.

  1. Honesty is one of the requirements in the job description: No one can test honesty, so listing it on a job description is kind of funny thing. I found these companies to be less transparent, actually. I guess they need more honest people because they are not.

  2. You cannot get any information on the company's salary range: if a company does does not ask for your desired/minimum salary, they will not pay you well, treat you well, and the company is doing bad. Don't think that you can convince them otherwise at the salary negotiation stage. Ask about it as soon as you are talking to a person.

  3. Any kind of lies and inconsistencies: don't think that, oh, "that is just an automated message." If you catch any lie or inconsistency in any description or words, you might end up losing your time, salary, or your health when you work with them.

  4. Salary suddenly drops during a negotiation: it would be more accurate to say that the company lied. For a contract position, $1 hourly wage equates to $1280 monthly salary and $16640 yearly salary.

  5. The company is hiring very frequently for its size: the turnover rate is high and people are leaving.

  6. They are being too nice: if they have time to write a nice personal email to you, they are not getting enough applicants. They are not getting enough applicants for the previous listed reasons. The more specialized / technical the career is, the job market is smarter. It adjusts amazing well to a company, both large and small. So these companies must opt to be nice, because it costs them least, in order to attract candidates.

  7. Company is referred by a recruiter with a cold email: A recruiter does not care about your interests, they are interested in getting a slice out of your employment by offering you jobs that are difficult and has a very high standard. I mean why else would a company turn to a recruiter? This will eventually turn into you wasting your time. If you applied directly to the recrutier after seeing the job description, that is a different story.

This one is a yellow flag: asking you to do a screening test even before a phone call: it's a bummer, because this should work beautifully in theory, but from my experience, I found these companies to be snobby and do not value your time. Most likely they are asking you to take these tests even without reading your resume. It is a different story though if they are actually talking to you, meaning there it is no longer an automated emails you are dealing with. I would just do these screen tests for my own enlightenment and so far they were indeed didactic.

Another note is that, just because the company email includes a personal contact information, that does not mean anything. Some does start revealing this info once they get serious, but some just reveal this straight from the beginning. It might be a way to fool you and get all serious and more committed.
