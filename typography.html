Topography Notes

August 22, 2013 [ web ] Read in 2 min.

Fundamentals of Design by Tim Dikun @ codeschool.com

Attributes that Influence Design

  • Audience: Age, Race, Gender, Cultural Niche
  • Tone: Casual, Formal, Friendly, Sarcastic
  • Purpose: Informational, Conversations

Serif

Humanist - Classic & Traditional

  • Emulates calligraphy
  • Contrasting Strokes
  • Journalist and Historical Text

Transitional - Strong, Stylish, & Dynamic

  • Sharper Serif
  • More contrasting Strokes
  • Academic and Legal

Modern Serif - Structured, Clear, & Elegant

  • Thin, horizontal Serif
  • High Contrast Stroke
  • Art and Cultural

Egyptian (Slab Serif) - Authoritative, yet friendly

  • Heavy, boxy, serifs
  • Very Low Contrast Stroke
  • Marketing and Promotional

Sans-Serif

Humanist - Perfect vs imperfect

  • Emulates calligraphy
  • contrasting strokes
  • Government, Finance, Education

Transitional - Unassuming & Modern

  • Strong strokes
  • Upright, uniform, characters
  • Technology and transportion

Geometric - Strict, Objective, & Universal

  • Geometric shape from the backbones of the letters

Script

  • Good for styling, but not for the body text.
  • including 'Comic Sans'

Guidelines

Titles and Subtitles: Serif Body: Sans serif Do not choose the fonts from the same class, but the same family. Seek for contrast over Harmony CSS

Design Hierarchy

  • Differences in Size
  • First choose the font for the Body. 16 px font size is a good rule of thumb
  • HeadLine(200%), Sub-Headline (150%), Navigation (100%), Subtitles & Annotation (75%)
  • Google leading is 120-150% 'line-height'
  • Good number of Characters-per-line (CPL) is 50-70, this may change if the device's viewing width is small. In css, this can be adjusted with max-width
