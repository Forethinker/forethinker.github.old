Porn is like cigarettes/cocaine to your psychological health.
June 27, 2014 [ life ] #security, #psychology, #habit Read in 3 min.

I never did drugs in my life, but I have seen their effects, so I am making the comparison. I am not the expert on the topic, but here is the best video I could find on the web: The Great Porn Experiment: Gary Wilson at TEDxGlasgow.

Once in my life, I thought I could never get away from it. I mean the pleasure (the release of dopamine) is just feels so irresistible because our brain is wired that way. But just as you are able to form a habit (addiction) to it, the steps also can be reversed. You can form a habit not to watch it. If you just rely on your will power, though, it will be like fighting Muhammad Ali after living like a couch potato for [time you have been watching porn]. I know because I have read numerous books to help me get out, and my father teaches at colleges and consult people who are on addiction. You can be a pastor, professor, or an athlete with a great will power but more likely than not you will lose. The only solution I found is to try to block it off completely. You will never get it right the first time, but don't give up and keep on blocking the Internet. You might not be able to block it right away, but the whole point is to make it as difficult as possible. Your brain is wired to do thing that are easy and get brainwashed easily by visual contents. The moment you lost and feel defeated and guilty, is the best time to devise a strategy for the next victory. It took me about five years, but only because I did not get my strategy right the first time. Once I had it down, as it I found a way to block porn and not everything else I need, it was about a three month long battle.

I think I am glad that I have tackled this early on in my life, because things are going to get more complicated. I have not tried to do self-parental control on my phone because I have not felt the need to, but I figure things could be a bit more troublesome.

If you don't stop it yourself, you will be forced to stop and you will be sorry. The other solution that the professor in the video mentions is to become erectilely dysfunctional. As a human being, I really want you to have a happy marriage. As a fellow society member, I really do not want to pay for your problems.

I have not found any source that says masturbation causes health problem. That is up to your philosophical / religious beliefs.

Lastly, if you have an idea that your girlfriend is your porn substitute, I would like you to consider otherwise for a long-term happiness. In college, my friends almost convinced by arguing that I need physical compatibility for a happy marriage. There is some truth to it, but it is not the complete picture. Our sex is much more psychological than physical when it comes to marriage.
