Minimalist Camera Setup
April 07, 2016 [ life ] #camera, #dslr, #lens Read in 2 min.

The argument for 35mm vs 50mm is endless and I finally came to peace with my choice. I own Pentax K-50, an excellent entry camera for a hobbyist. After playing around with the zoom lens kit (18-55), I bought my first prime lens with 55mm focal length. I did feel some uneasiness when I tried to take group photo, or any indoor photo that needed a bit more landscape. From the Pentax Forum, 2/3 of the users recommended 35mm.. I was a click away from buying 35mm lens, but then after some research. I decided to invest more money on my next cell phone. Cellphone cameras have 26-29mm equivalent focal length. So, let's consider the use cases for 35mm lens:

  • 35mm: photos which include the environment with the subject; groups of people; use in a tight space; or landscape photography.

The use cases matches cellphone pretty well. You would usually share these with friends and cell phone has sharing ability to make these happen quickly. I do think I can skip DSLR for these photos. But I could also now see why the people on the forum suggested 35mm. It is the focal length people are most comfortable with.

Now let's look at the use cases for 50mm:

  • 50mm: one person photos; keeping a little distance; and more bokeh.

I would say you need DSLR for these. These are special pictures that need a bit more of attention and post-processing. If you want to read more about the difference, here is a good comparison between 35mm and 50mm in depth. If I am out in a good weather and there is a possibility of water, I could opt for zoom lens since I don't have to worry too much about the aperture and forego cell phone camera.

With a decent cellphone and DSLR + 50mm, most use cases will be covered. Yes, some scenario will not be covered, a group photo in a low-light setting, for instance, but I think this is a nice compromise. In conclusion, I was forgetting to utilize cellphone camera thinking that it is just inferior to DSLR. My current camera is a bit on the low-tier side, but it is definitely underutilized.

TLDR: cell phone for wide, DSLR for macro, zoom lens for good weather/waterproof
