The backup software
December 02, 2013 [ hack ] #backup Read in 3 min.

I have used Acrnois Backup (2007) and Easeus Backup (2013). They both have failed me. The failures were the same even though they are five years apart in the time period - bootdisk/recovery OS failure. Macrium Reflect Free's recovery disk worked and it supported USB 3.0, but then it failed to deal with GPT partitions that Microsoft introduced with Windows 8. Paragon Hard Disk Manager did the job and it always have been in the top picks in the reviews that I wanted to try, so I finally had a winner.

The Most Disastrous Moment:

Computer backup is the insurance that you cannot outsource. So it is a hassle, no matter how automated it is, and it makes you become lazy about it. I have gotten lazy at point and I paid it dearly. I was trying to encrypt my external drive that has all my data without backing it up first. Since Truecrypt is a trusted software, I thought, it should be pretty safe as long as I am not doing anything. Well, not doing anything actually caused the problem. My laptop went to sleep mode while encryption was happening and truecrypt bricked my backup drive. And when your hard drive is half encrypted with a broken header, there is no way to recover it.

It is a pretty embarrassing story, but nonetheless I still get lazy about backup at times. Like when one software fails me and I have to switch over, or I move and the internet bandwidth is not good enough for off-site backup, or when my computer dies, etc, etc.

GPT UUFI

It is one mysterious beast that I do not have time to tame. I am so mad that it dethroned Macrium Reflect Free and forced me to use a paid software. I wish well for backup software companies, but whenever there is a migration or re-installation, the license validation becomes a chore. Here are some commands I tried on command-line on Windows 8 recovery disc, but did not fix the problem:

Diskpart
List volume
Select volume=2 (replace “2″ with the number that is associated with the “ESP” label)
assign letter=Z
exit
bcdboot C:\windows /s Z: /f UEFI (replace C: with the volume letter that is named “OS”)

Robocopy

robocopy c:\ d:\ /MIR /R:3 /W:0
/MIR = Mirror entire directory structure
/R:0 = 0 retries for read/write failures
/W:0 = 0 seconds between retries
