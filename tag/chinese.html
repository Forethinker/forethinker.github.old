Learning Chinese
October 30, 2013 [ life ] #language, #chinese, #learning Read in 3 min.

Why learn Mandarin?

China's economy is gaining momentum and there are a lot of people who will use Mandarin. Frankly, I studied Spanish for four years and I still do not think I do not have positive ROI of my time. I managed to get better services and find my way in Los Angeles downtown with my rusty Spanish, but I never had to speak the language in social settings or at work. When I was in college I met a lot of Chinese people and I felt left out whenever I joined their social group. Because East Asia countries are very ethnocentric, it is really difficult to build strong bonds in relationship without knowing the language.

I was the president of Chinese Language Club in high school, but my effort to learn Mandarin never lasted long. So now I am going for long-term (10-years) and no-pressure approach. My current setup is: Anki - vocabulary. There are decks with speech too. Pleco - input writing came free with the promotion and I paid for the stroke order diagram. It is really money well spent. I am going to for traditional characters because my plan is to learn Mandarin over next 10 years. * Learn Mandarin: Fluenz Mandarin 1+2+3 with supplemental Audio CDs and Podcasts - I think I learn better with female instructor. I kid. Their emphasis on typing the intonation after listening, encouraging to memorize the dialog are big pluses. You get the feeling of using a start-up company's beta software in comparison to say, Rosetta Stone, but the product lives up to Amazon.com's user rating in terms of knowledge retention. It is pricey though. See if you snag one on eBay.com.

Other Resources:

  • Learn radicals, common characters
  • Read chinese ([Chinese Haacks][http://chinesehacks.com/])
  • Watch children's cartoon 喜洋洋与灰太狼
  • Watch Chinese TV shows (sugoideas.com
  • Use QQ and RenRen - to communicate with Chinese people and practice in real world settings. Most Chinese people I met graciously responded in English when I could not continue.
  • Read Chinese News (Sohu BBC weibo)
  • Use Chinese search engine (Baidu)
  • Hire Chinese tutor and read a book together

Programs:

  • Google translate
  • Hanping for Android and Pleco for iOS.
  • Supposedly Hanping > Pleco, but you be the judge.
  • Fluent U
  • skritter and Skritter Chinese in iOS for writing practice
