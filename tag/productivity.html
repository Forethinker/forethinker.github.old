Single Monitor rules
October 11, 2013 [ life ] #productivity Read in 2 min.

No matter how good of a peripheral vision you have, you cannot possibly focus on entire surface of 24" monitor. So why then would you want a big monitor or even multiple monitors? Because you have a less-than-optimal setup where turning your head is faster than switching tasks with your hands. If you want to be an expert, you got to become a shortcut-foo master. Just like an expert pianists do not rely on their eyes but their senses, your computer and you must become one via keyboard. Muscle memory is much more efficient than a computer. I have written about my shortcut setup in another article and with that setup, you should be fine with just one monitor. You should be able to switch to tasks using muscle memory rather than twitching your head like a dove. I have used multiple monitor before, but with the frequency of my task switching, I get sores all over my neck, and my irritating neck ruins my mood and patience to get my work done.

Screen resolution, size went up and the price went down over the years, but for me the ratio comes up as a requirement. This is because there are a limit number of models that defy the conventional 16:9 widescreen display.

I am currently using Dell UltraSharp 24" IPS panel which has 1920x1200 resolution. That extra 180 vertical pixel comes very handy. I recommend it wholeheartedly. I think review stars will mean more to you than my endorsement. I found >4 stars everywhere and I think it is best one for the specification I am looking for.

I think it is better than to invest money on a side-kick laptop that you could keep you productive while your main computer is busy.
