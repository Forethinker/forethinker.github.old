Zenburn to Solarized
September 30, 2013 [ hack ] #design Read in 2 min.

![Alt Natural Ligtht Color Chart](|filename|/images/natural-light.jpg "Better than Sunshine: See Life in an Improved Light"

I switched to solarized color theme while installing airilne. Solarized is a very beautiful theme. I am partial to darker version, but light version is fine too. They both give modern looks, but the darker version, however, is more comfortable to my eyes. I would say Zenburn is still good if you are about to go to bed, because that orangish color is supposed to relax you while bluish colors excites you. Anyway, I knew solarized was a popular choice, but like Jamie Curle here I thought it was a bit too radical for me first. After two hours of using it, I decided to adapt it everywhere I can. Good thing is that there are a lot fans of Solarized and hence many resources are already available if you look around.. Here is one for terminator (https://github.com/ghuntley/terminator-solarized/blob/master/config)

Solarized + Consolas + Vim-Airline go really well together. I dare say Triforce of Vim's Modern Style. I am very grateful for people who strive for beauty and humanistic aspects of computing. It makes my time on computer so much more enjoyable. It gives that feeling of walking in a garden in a sunny day.

Give Solarized a try. I think you will like it within a couple of hours.

You can see the exact steps I took to install it in cygwin in another article.

I installed Solarized Theme on Powershell using the registry values as compiled by nelipa. Make sure to back up your HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Console value though, as his script does not provide you with a rollback registry. Mine can be downloaded here. It is based on Windows 7 Service Pack 1 x64. You will have to reboot to see the effect. Check your color scheme with:

0..15 | % { Write-Host -ForegroundColor $_ "$_" }

If you are using Gnome-terminal, the color scheme of the terminal matters alot:

http://unix.stackexchange.com/questions/66579/how-do-i-get-the-solarized-colour-scheme-working-with-gnome-terminal-tmux-and-v
