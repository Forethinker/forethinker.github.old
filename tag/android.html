What I want in a phone
January 02, 2016 [ life ] #phone, #android, #google, #features, #hardware Read in 4 min.

Wanted Features

  • stock Android: Google's Android features get updated very frequently. A lot has change since I first got Nexus 5 and it is still updating earlier than other brands'. Software is the heart of a machine and I would rather choose stock Android even if I have to sacrifice other features.
  • fast charging: I am waiting for Snapdragon 820 really badly right now. Reportedly, Huawei is making a successor to Nexus 6P in 2016 with snapdragon 820. I think this is going my next phone.
  • replaceable battery: with the fast charging and a bigger battery, the need for a replaceable battery will be gone, but upgradability still matters. The battery health will give in after a year or two. Especially with the phone becoming a commodity and much more durable, I might end up using my next phone for 4 years.
  • physical buttons: I was a fan of utilizing the entire screen of a phone, but when I used Samsung Note 5, I found that sometimes these buttons are not responsive. I found a few others complaining about it on the web. If the manufacturer can get this right and it can be used comfortably with one hand, then it would be great. But I could live without it.

Meh Features

  • Big screen: it really depends on the ergonomics. But I see both pros and cons in screen sizes. I am happy with either, but I'd opt for something in between like 5.3"
  • Rear buttons: if it works well, I am okay with it
  • USB-C: this does not make that much difference in my work flow
  • Aluminum bodies: it is nice to have, but I'd rather have a rugged design like LG V10 so that I don't have to put an extra case.
  • Samsung Pay: it is a nice to have, but I'd rather hand over my card than my phone when I go through a drive-through
  • speakers: I am usually at a public place, so I don't need any good speakers
  • Wireless charging: although I really cannot use my phone properly while it is being charge. This can be great if I use it in my car, but the charging efficiency is still a problem. The power draw of GPS will drain the battery despite the wireless charging.
  • Stylus:

Noway Features

  • Curved display: this makes sense for a big monitor or a TV, not so much for a phone. A foldable phone makes sense.
  • Curved edge: it is a marketing gimmick that kills usability. How are you suppose to use a phone properly when you need a good grip?
  • Secondary screen: this means non-stock android. I want to avoid proprietary features as much as possible. Also there is a good chance that it won't be good because not many softwares utilize it.
  • SD card: I thought I needed this. But I don't after removing data cap on my phone. I stream everything now and it is more convenient that way. If I were to use my phone for a different purpose or use it for ROM testing, I might need it, but I am going to use my phone as a phone, not as a computer or a server.

Ubiquitous features in 2016

  • Fingerprint sensors: the compatibility with lastpass is great. Also no need for a gesture password!
  • camera quality: I realized that this is very important to capture precious moments in life. Most flagships' camera quality are good, so I am less worried about this now.

Others

  • Unlimited Data (~6GB): it is amazing when you can thether and start working on your laptop. Also a lot of hassles of moving pictures, video, and music can be gone if you start embracing streaming.
  • Coverage: Verizon dominates in this. If you have an international phone. Having an ability to switch to a SIM card that utilizes Verizon network would be great when you go travelling into the wild.
  • Bluetooth headset: I haven't found one with a good mouthpiece, and charging is another task to do everyday. I ditch blutooth headset for a wired one. It can be useful when I am running, but that is about it. I am okay with wired one in any situation
