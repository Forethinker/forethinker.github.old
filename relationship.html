Construction Communication Tactics in Relationship

November 19, 2015 [ life ] Read in 2 min.

Notes from the book "Our Sexuality" by Robert L. Crooks

Gottman's Constructive Communication Tactics

  • Leveling: stating our thought and feelings clearly, simply, and honestly: preferably using "I" language
  • Editing: avoid saying things that are hurtful and expressing only the information that are relevant to the issues
  • Validating: facilitating constructive dialogue by acknowledging the reasonableness of our partner's concern.
  • Volatile Dialogue: some degree of conflict is essential to the long-term happiness of a relationship. For a long-term relationship (> 3 years), the couple who argued occasionally reported significantly more relationship satisfaction.

Gottman's Destructive Communication Tactics

  • Criticism: "you" statements that attack's one's character instead of addressing the issue at hand.
  • Contempt: conveying emotions and thought via insults, sarcasm, and name calling.
  • Defensiveness: excuses, denying responsibility, replying back with a criticism
  • Stonewalling: silence, avoidance, ignoring

  • Belligerence: provoking style of interaction

  • Love triangle theory: passion, intimacy, and commitment

  • Six different love styles: romantic, game playing, possessive, compassionate, altruistic, and pragmatic
  • The transition from passion to deep attraction result from gradual increase of endorphins in the brain.
  • Factors for interpersonal attractions are: similarity, reciprocity, and physical attractiveness.
  • Parents play a great role in shaping their children's social competence and relationship skills. Securely attached adults can build stable and satisfying relationships and do not fear of being abandoned because they have a good self-image.
