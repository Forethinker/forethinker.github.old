Desktop Customization is a waste of time

September 15, 2013 [ life ] #design, #complaints Read in 4 min.

There are designs that are useful, and there are designs that are unnecessary. When desiging something, let each element have a purpose and serve it well. Making a "in-between" design might make it a good marketing-gimick, but it will end up being a waste of time. Yes there is an exception to this rule, like a big phone, but I think it is very difficult to make the case for desktop customization. I would even argue that desktop customization as a harem of design (notorious MySpace customization back in 2007 fall under this category). It's flashy and desriable, but it is often morally wrong because it eats up your time and brain power for no good reason.

Weather

I always found morning sky to be a better indicator than the cute looking ones on PDAs, Desktops, etc. If I really wanted to know about the weather, I would just make a browser shortcut so that I can see the detailed report on it.

Desktop Background Image

I am curious how much time you spend staring at that image. The time I spent looking at my desktop accounts for less than 1% of my computer usage. And if I am looking at it, it is because I need to see the icons on my desktop. I do not want the desktop image to interfere with the view of icons on there, so I keep it pretty monotone. syn match myExCapitalWords +\<\w[A-Z]\K>+ contains=@NoSpell

Hardware stats

Okay, this can be useful, but still unnecessary. I could think of a three scenario when looking at these numbers: 1) Your hardware load is low - you lost a second of your life needlessly and you move on. 2) Your hardware load is high - you need to figure out why, you either pullout Windows Task Manager or htop to kill the troublemaker. 3) You are trying to see whether your latest installation or action is causing a spike in the process. I could easily tell if my system is under load by the latency I experience, when that happens, I pull out WTM or htop right away. It gives me all the details I need right away. Once again I am better off without it.

Custom Icons / No icons

Okay it might give you that 'Zen' feeling as you stare at that uncluttered page. I use web browser's new tab page for this purpose. I do not need to customize this one.

Newsfeed

If you enjoy reading introduction of articles, this is for you. It's not for me. RSS readers are much better optimized for reading experience and saving your time.

Todo-list

I just use vim for this. Much faster to find what you need and edit it on the spot, in a vim way.

The only real optimization I do is the color scheme, because darker color is much easier on my eyes. But even here, I just use a theme someone created, so it is a one-click deal. I do not look for other themes because the current one serves its purpose well. Another exception I would take is a vocabulary card on your desktop. I say exception, because if I am ever looking at my desktop, I am in a middle of doing something. I really do not want something trivial to make me lose focus. I have done something like this on my terminal with GRE words, but it was not that very helpful. Whenever you look at some notification, you brain is making a context-switch. And we know that context-switch is not a cheap operation.

So all in all, I would say desktop customization could create more distraction than improve productivity, and that defeats the purpose of a good design. I am a type of a person who finds a cartoon on an exam, for example, distracting, so I am an extremist. But the takeaway here is that do not get jealous when someone has a flashy desktop or an office space. Simpler is better.
