My Choice of Hardware in 2016
June 06, 2016 [ life ] Read in 2 min.

Phone | Nexus 5

Still running strong after three years. I am waiting for Nexus 2016 with Android N. My folks are still using Nexus 5 with no problem. They will probably get a refresh once Nexus 2016 price drops in about a year after the release. Nexus 6P dropped about 33% in price after 9 months of release. I can't wait for my phone to replace my credit cards.

Camera | Pentax K-50

Weatherproof and best bang for the buck in the entry-level camera. I use 50mm prime with it.

Laptop | Dell M3800

The best laptop I used and nothing else compares to it. The CPU power has not improved much since 2013. The introduction of Sandy Bridge was huge, (Ivy Bridge, tick) the next tock Haswell was a pretty good improvement in both performance and energy consumption. (Broadwell, tick) But Skylake only offers slight improvement over its predecessors, where the upgrade is not as significant to justify the upgrade. It comes with HDMI 2.0 for 4k 60hz, DDR4, USB-C. But I see them as nice to haves instead of necessities.

Server | Synology

Once you use it, you can't never go back. It is a pleasure to use, low maintenance, and reliable.

Monitor | LG IPS236-PN 23"

I tired Dell 34 inch Ultrawide monitor (U3415W). If you are adept with keyboard shortcuts and have the windows management system ready, 34 inch monitor is more of a distraction than a productivity enhancer. My current monitor + laptop screen provide plenty of screen estate for me. I think 1080p is the standard for most places, so connecting and disconnecting external monitors do not disarrange windows placement. Also having HDMI and audio output is great so that I could have a dedicated speakers / earphones for my home setup.

Power Bank | DBPower DJS20

12000mah battery bank to power my phones, gadgets, laptop, and even car battery in case of an emergency.
