Don't give up on your anonymity

August 12, 2013 [ life ] #career Read in 3 min.

"People behave a lot better when they have their real names down. I think people hide behind anonymity and they feel like they can say whatever they want behind closed doors." - Randi Zuckerberg (Sister of Mark Zuckerberg)

I agree with everything she says, but I endorse anonymity in a learning/development/professional environment. I have a lot of people who holds me accountable for what I do. Good thing is that they do not write down everything I say or do. The Internet can. The internet is a difficult place to please everyone and I think that you can lose your identity amidst of all the information and opinions.

In short, I want the freedom of going back to my cave whenever I feel like it. When I stop blogging, I do not want people to think I have a personal problem, etc. When I stop contributing, I do not want to feel guilt. It is unnecessary stress that does not help anyone. Also when I try new things, I DO want people to treat me like a novice, be more gracious with my mistakes, and do tell me the details. I also want others to approach me without prejudice. I make silly mistakes because it just happens, not because I want it to. And I do not want to feel stupid about it or fear that it will bring me down in the future. I can be more honest when I am anonymous and thus be more productive as a developer. I already use a lot of my brain power when I interact on the internet. I do not want the perfectionist side of me to take over by revealing my identity.

Imagine a database professor who wants to learn about computer graphics and joins stackoverflow. Do you think he feels easy about asking a simple question? Obviously no. And what if he makes a mistakes and someone points that out to him? Haha, I could totally imagine the guy trying to wriggle his way out without admiting his fault. Well I do not have that much accountability on my job title, but who knows what will happen in the future? With anonymity, I can admit my mistake whenever I want without pressure and that helps everybody.

Maybe you are a type of person who needs the motivation to better behave, but I doubt it. If you are debating between anonymity and your real name. I recommend you to choose the former and you have more options later.

"Your identity is your most valuable possession. Protect it. And if anything goes wrong, use your powers." - Violet Parr (from the movie called Incredibles)

No you are not a superhero, but you can be darn close to one with your computer.
